Nescafé vs. Starbucks: Market Affinity Analysis
Project Overview
This project uses Competitive Indexing to move beyond simple sales metrics and understand the psychological and demographic drivers behind Nescafé and Starbucks. By analyzing factors like Age Brackets and Shopping Attitudes, we identify how Nescafé can pivot from a "budget" perception to an "Accessible Status" symbol.

Problem Statement
The goal is to provide a data-driven strategy to capture under-indexed segments (such as Retired and Highly Educated cohorts) while leveraging Nescafé's current dominance in Large Households (6-7+ people) and Gen Z segments.

Project Pipeline
Segment Analysis: Classified consumer data into Core (Over-Index), Risk (Under-Index), and Average tiers.
Influential Touchpoints: Analyzed the impact of physical versus digital marketing, identifying a massive lead for Nescafé in Special Events (Index 700).
Behavioral Mapping: Linked demographic traits like education to shopping mindsets to uncover the Value-Status Paradox.

Key Results & Insights
Status-Driven Loyalty: Nescafé loyalists are not budget-focused; they are primarily Status-driven (Index ~725) and Values-driven (Index ~350).
Physical Dominance: Nescafé outperforms Starbucks in real-world visibility, particularly in Outdoor Advertising (Index >400).
Digital Vulnerability: A critical gap exists in digital convenience, where Delivery perceptions index as low as 13.

Conclusions
The analysis demonstrates that Nescafé’s future growth lies in Premium Innovation. By addressing the Digital Convenience gap and targeting specialized dietary needs (Vegan/Grain-free), the brand can successfully attract the affluent, highly-educated segments currently favoring Starbucks.
